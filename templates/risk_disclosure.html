The risk of loss in trading futures, options, and other derivative products can be substantial. Before trading, you should carefully consider:

2. Futures Trading Risks

Futures trading carries specific risks that you should understand:

2.1 Leverage Risk

Futures are highly leveraged instruments. A small market movement can result in large losses (or gains) relative to your initial margin deposit. You may be required to deposit additional funds on short notice to maintain your position, or your position may be liquidated at a loss.

2.2 Market Volatility

Futures markets can be extremely volatile. Prices can move rapidly and unpredictably due to:

  • Economic news and data releases
  • Geopolitical events
  • Market sentiment changes
  • Technical factors and algorithmic trading
  • Liquidity conditions

2.3 Gap Risk

Markets may "gap" - opening at a significantly different price than the previous close. This can result in losses exceeding your stop-loss orders.

2.4 Liquidity Risk

In certain market conditions, it may be difficult or impossible to execute trades at desired prices, or to close out positions.

4. Automated Trading Risks

Using automated trading systems, including Just.Trades., involves additional specific risks:

4.1 System Failures

  • Software Bugs: No software is perfect. Bugs may cause unintended trades or prevent intended trades.
  • Connectivity Issues: Internet outages, broker API failures, or server problems may prevent trade execution or cause delays.
  • Hardware Failures: Server or infrastructure failures may disrupt trading operations.

4.2 Strategy Risks

  • Past Performance: Past performance of any strategy is not indicative of future results.
  • Market Regime Changes: A strategy that worked in the past may fail in different market conditions.
  • Overfitting: Strategies may be optimized for historical data but fail in live trading.
  • Slippage: Actual execution prices may differ from expected prices, affecting profitability.

4.3 Monitoring Requirements

Even with automated trading, you should:

  • Regularly monitor your positions and account
  • Verify that the system is operating correctly
  • Be prepared to intervene manually if necessary
  • Not assume the system will always work as expected

We do not guarantee that our platform will be available 100% of the time, that trades will always execute successfully, or that any strategy will be profitable.
